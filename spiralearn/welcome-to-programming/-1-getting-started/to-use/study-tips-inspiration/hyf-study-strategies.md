# Study Strategies

It's hard to study effectively. This is mostly because we've never really
learned how to study the right way, even after years of university in some
cases! In this chapter we will show you some of the most important strategies to
learn in a way that is much more time efficient.

As the HackYourFuture course is very demanding, this is critical to get through
the program successfully. In fact, many successful experts that require focus
and concentration for their craft (programmers, writers, artists, scientists,
craftsmen, etc.) share one thing in common: they are very serious about how they
learn. This chapter will give you a short introduction into how you can study
most effectively.

## Planning

The first part of studying should be to make a study plan. Your homework will
consist of various components. You will need to read/watch a certain amount of
information, and apply this knowledge in the homework exercises.

Every week consists of the homework of the previous week and the reading
material for the following week. Before you start doing anything, it's good to
plan your week ahead.

## Don't just read, code it

Reading about code, or listening to your teacher about code, is not the same as
actually studying code! It is incredibly important that you try out the concepts
that you have learned, and that you try to apply it in multiple ways in order to
become comfortable with it.

We recommend that when you study something, directly try it out in your code
editor by running the examples and writing your own examples and see how it
works in practice.

One more tip: Write code with pen and paper. Research shows that writing things
down with good old-fashioned pen and paper provides cognitive benefits that you
cannot achieve when typing on your keyboard.

## Group Study

Learning and programming are both less fun alone. Planning group study sessions
will make your time more fun and more productive.

One great way to work together is _Pair Programming_, this is a technique where
two people code together on a single computer.

## Sleep, Breaks and Exercise

Sleep is incredibly important for your brain's memory capability. If you study a
lot, but don't sleep enough, part of all the hours you put in are useless. On
average the recommended time of sleep is ~8 hours.

Try to take 10-15 minutes breaks in between studying sessions of 90 minutes.
During these breaks, some form of physical exercise is highly recommended.

## Distractions

The goal whilst studying should be to reach a state of 'flow'. Flow can be
described as the complete immersion in an activity.

### Challenge number 1: Social Media & Phone

Social media is incredibly bad for your focus. When you are studying, put your
phone away and use your study time for studying only.

### Challenge number 2: Children and family

Try to create a quiet space, where you will not be interrupted. Perhaps you can
ask your partner or a friend to keep an eye on your children for a few hours.

## How to become a pro at learning

An excellent read about the value of working without distractions is the book
Deep Work by Cal Newport.
