# Find Your Why

Learning to code demands significant time and persistent effort. Feeling
confident means you're not pushing hard enough.

Your motivation source matters less than its emotional resonance. Whether driven
by natural talent, peer influence, career advancement, or genuine passion, you
must connect deeply with your purpose and revisit it during difficult moments.

Write a personal mission statement for learning to code and put it somewhere
visible — your GitHub bio, your portfolio, your bathroom mirror, your computer
background.

## The Framework

When debugging frustrations emerge, remember this structure:

- **What**: Programming itself
- **How**: Study and practice methods
- **Why**: Your foundational motivation

Programming may suit some people better as a hobby than a profession. Conduct
thorough industry research before committing.
