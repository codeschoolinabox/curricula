# Help Your Brain

Effective learning is (very roughly) built of these main pieces:

- **Accurate Meta-Cognition**: Self-learning is also self-teaching. Accurately
  assessing your abilities (positive and negative) will make all the difference.
  - [Tips for Improving](http://www.learningscientists.org/blog/2017/3/30-1)

- **Concentrated and Intentional Effort**: You will learn most effectively when
  you are deliberate about every action you take.
  - [Practicing Deliberate Practice](https://medium.com/the-crossover-cast/get-better-at-anything-6-steps-of-deliberate-practice-19830bfc9460)

- **Cognitive Load Management**: Managing this is so important for learning
  because you most effectively learn information that has been fully processed
  by your working memory.
  - _Intrinsic Load_: The inherent complexity of what you are trying to learn.
  - _Extraneous Load_: Anything unrelated to the concept you are trying to
    learn.
  - _Germane Load_: The good one — the work your brain does that translates
    directly into meaningful learning.
  - [Great Overview of Cognitive Load](https://www.mindtools.com/pages/article/cognitive-load-theory.htm)

- **Forming & Maintaining Quality Mental Models**: A sign of effective learning
  is automatization — that lovely moment where you no longer need to think to
  complete a task.
  - Explain everything back to yourself.
  - Draw diagrams of how you think things work.
  - Find connections between what you're learning and what you already know.
  - Ask for help early and often.
  - [What are Mental Models?](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5K_sxBUGx8A)
  - [Make a Concept Map](https://www.wikihow.com/Make-a-Concept-Map)
  - [In-Depth](https://www.fs.blog/mental-models/)

In short, your brain wants to learn. Give it a fighting chance!
