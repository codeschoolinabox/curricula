# Structure Your Inquiry

> "Tell me and I forget, show me and I remember, involve me and I understand."

Learning is an active verb. Forming and exploring the right questions is our
favorite way of interacting with new challenges.

## Be Curious

Questions only matter if you try to find the answer. Staying curious ties in
very closely with motivation.

## Be Playful

Turn everything you do into a little bit of a game, puzzle, or work of art.

## Be Cautious

Unstructured learning is dangerous to meaningful learning. It's very easy to
learn incorrect habits, plausible but incorrect mental models, or completely
miss the point.

## Be Deliberate

Not all questions are created equal. Find the best way to phrase your question:

- Predictive & Falsifiable?
- Multiple Choice?
- Open-Ended?
- True/False?

Constrain your exploration:

- "If I come across hoisting I'll back off"
- "After 5 minutes I'm moving on"
- "I don't need to understand this concept yet, just use it"

Identify the level of understanding you have or want:

- Facts, Vocabulary, Explicit procedures, Abstract procedures, Conversational,
  Could teach it, Could reconstruct it

Ask Meta-Questions:

- Why did I like this question better?
- Can this question be answered definitively?
- What level of abstraction am I working in?

## Be Strategic

Connect your questions into a larger and coherent story. Diversify your
questions. Find relationships between them. Coordinate them into a study plan.
